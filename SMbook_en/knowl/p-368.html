The word constraint is used to indicate a device that limits the possibilities of movement of a system of bodies. A generic system, for example a system of rigid bodies, is described by \(n\) parameters, \(q_1, \dots q_n \text{,}\) which are called Lagrangian coordinates of the system or degrees of freedom. In these terms, the generic constraint condition is expressed, in the most generic form, in the following way:

\begin{equation} f\left[q_1, \dots, q_n, \dot{q}_1, \dots, \dot{q}_n, t\right] \leq 0.\tag{4.2.1} \end{equation}

Where, in addition to the Lagrangian coordinates, their derivatives also appear, \(\dot{q}_1, \dots, \dot{q} _n \text{,}\) with respect to time.
