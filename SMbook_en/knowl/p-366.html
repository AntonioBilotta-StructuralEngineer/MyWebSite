An alternative writing of the equation (4.1.4) is obtained by making explicit the structure of the tensor \(\text{skew} \tens{\nabla u} \text{,}\) that is

\begin{equation} \vec{u} = \vec{u}_o + \varphi \,\tens{W}\left(\vec{X}-\vec{X}_o\right)\,,\tag{4.1.6} \end{equation}

where \(\varphi\) is the angle of rotation and \(\tens{W} \) is a generic skew tensor, see InsightÂ 1.9.5 . Furthermore, using the axial vector associated with the tensor \(\tens{W} \text{,}\) it can also be written

\begin{equation} \vec{u} = \vec{u}_o + \varphi\, \vec{\omega}\times\left(\vec{X}-\vec{X}_o\right)\,.\tag{4.1.7} \end{equation}
