\begin{equation*} f \,:\, x \in \mathbb{R} \,\longmapsto\, y \in \mathbb{R} \end{equation*}
\begin{align*} dy \amp = \func{f}{x+dx} - \func{f}{x}\\ \amp = \func{f}{x} +\frac{df}{dx}\,dx + \func{\omega}{dx} - \func{f}{x}\\ \amp =\frac{df}{dx}\,dx + \func{\omega}{dx} \end{align*}

where \(\func{\omega}{dx}/dx \to 0\) if \(dx \to 0\text{,}\) then it is possible to state that

\begin{equation} dy = \frac{df}{dx}\,dx\,.\tag{1.3.1} \end{equation}
