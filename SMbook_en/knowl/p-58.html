This result shows how the transition from \(d\vec{X}\) to \(d\vec{x}\) occurs through a linear transformation represented by the matrix \(\mat{F}\) which, for each point of the domain, contains all the information needed to characterize the deformation. \(\tens{F}\) is named as deformation gradient. On thsi basis the relationship between the differentials, \(d\vec{X}\) and \(d\vec{x}\text{,}\) can be expressed in compact form as

\begin{equation} d\vec{x} = \tens{F}\, d\vec{X}\,.\tag{1.3.9} \end{equation}

Relationship that can be specified more precisely as follows

\begin{equation} d\vec{x} = \func{\tens{F}}{\vec{X}}\, d\vec{X}\,,\tag{1.3.10} \end{equation}

where the dependence of \(\tens{F}\) on the point where the gradient is evaluated has been made explicit.
