The one-dimensional formulation of the internal work (3.5.1) becomes

\begin{align*} L_i \amp = \int_{\body} \sigma\varepsilon \, dV\\ \amp = \int_{l}\int_{A} \sigma \left(- y \frac{d^2 w}{dx^2} \right) \, dS \,dx\\ \amp = \int_{l}\int_{A} \sigma\, y \, dS \left(- \frac{d^2 w}{dx^2} \right) \,dx\\ \amp = \int_{l} M \left(- \frac{d^2 w}{dx^2} \right) \,dx\,,\\ \amp = \int_{l} M \chi \,dx\,, \end{align*}

therefore

\begin{equation} L_i = \int_{l} M \chi \,dx\,.\tag{3.6.7} \end{equation}

Where

\begin{equation} M =\int_{A} \sigma\,y \, dS\,,\tag{3.6.8} \end{equation}

is the bending moment which is the static entity of the one-dimensional model, and

\begin{equation} \chi = - \frac{d^2 w}{dx^2}\,,\tag{3.6.9} \end{equation}

represents the curvature, the kinematic entity work-conjugate with \(M\text{.}\) The measure for \(M\) is \(\left[\text{F}\,\text{L}\right]\) and for \(\chi\) is \(\left[1/\text{L}\right]\text{.}\)
