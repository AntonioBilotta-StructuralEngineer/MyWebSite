The presence of the roller support determines, from the static point of view, only a reaction force, \(\vec{R} \text{,}\) directed according to the axis of the roller support. Then, of the three reaction components, only the first two can be different from zero

\begin{equation} H \neq 0 \,, \quad V \neq 0 \,,\quad \mathcal{M} = 0\,.\tag{5.3.2} \end{equation}

Furthermore, the direction of the reaction force is known and therefore the two components \(H \) and \(V \) are not independent of each other but linked by the relationship

\begin{equation} \frac{V}{H} = \tan{\alpha}\,.\tag{5.3.3} \end{equation}
