The roller support dictates that the displacement of the point where it is applied can only occur in the direction orthogonal to the axis of the roller support:

\begin{equation} \vec{u}_A \cdot \vec{n} = 0\tag{4.2.3} \end{equation}

where

\begin{equation*} \vec{u}_A = \left[\begin{array}{c} u_{1A} \\ u_{2A}\end{array}\right] \,, \quad \vec{n} = \left[\begin{array}{c} \cos{\alpha} \\ \sin{\alpha}\end{array}\right] \end{equation*}

In the particular case in which the axis is horizontal, \(\alpha=0\) or \(\alpha=\pi\text{,}\) condition (4.2.3) gives

\begin{equation} u_{1A} = 0\,.\tag{4.2.4} \end{equation}

If the axis is vertical, \(\alpha=\pi/2\) or \(\alpha=-\pi/2\text{,}\) the constraint condition becames

\begin{equation} u_{2A} = 0\,.\tag{4.2.5} \end{equation}
